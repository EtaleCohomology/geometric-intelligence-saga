# Resonance

*On the structural resonance between Geometric Intelligence (GI) theory and the great traditions of twentieth-century thought.*

## On the Category

The present subdirectory gathers essays devoted to what the author designates as *structural resonance* (Strukturresonanz): the relation, neither of translation nor of formalisation nor of reduction, that obtains between the mathematical phenomena described by GI theory and the phenomena described, in quite other languages, by traditions of thought that have addressed the temporal and spatial structure of human experience. The essays in this directory do not attempt to translate Bergsonian duration, Heideggerian being-in-the-world, Kimura's "in-between," or Corbin's history of sensibilities into the vocabulary of differentiable manifolds and Lie derivatives. What they attempt is something more modest and at the same time more exacting: to listen for the points at which the mathematical apparatus of GI theory and the conceptual apparatuses of these traditions, despite their radical heterogeneity, indicate the same structural phenomenon — each in its own idiom, from its own vantage, without loss of its own dignity.

The methodological stance is therefore that of the listener rather than that of the translator. The author neither claims that the Lie derivative captures the essence of pure duration nor that the geodesic distance on a Riemannian manifold reconstitutes Kimura's *aida*; such claims would be, and would rightly be taken as, a form of reductive hubris. What is affirmed is only that beneath the incommensurable surfaces of these disciplines there runs a subterranean communication, and that the task of the present essays is to attend to that communication wherever it can be heard.

The category is conceived as an open-ended series. Further essays — on Gadamer's horizon-fusion and the historical manifold, on Cassirer's symbolic forms and the dimensionality of the latent space, on Simondon's individuation and the dynamics of the Lie bracket, and on other such possible resonances — will be added as the programme unfolds.

## Contents

### `01_duration_and_inbetween/`

*The Geometry of Duration and the In-Between: Bergson — Heidegger/Zollikon — Kimura/Kyōto — Corbin/Annales — and GI Theory.*

The inaugural essay of the category. A working paper tracing the structural resonances between, on the one hand, Bergson's pure duration, Heidegger's being-in-the-world and the Zollikon seminars, Kimura Bin's *aida* and the Kyōto School, and Corbin's history of sensibilities; and, on the other, the differentiable manifold, the Lie derivative, scalar curvature, Morse functions, and geodesic distance of GI theory. The essay is framed by Nishida Kitarō's "absolutely contradictory self-identity" and closes with reflections upon the Langlands programme, Maruyama Yoshihiro's categorical unified science, and Mochizuki's inter-universal Teichmüller theory as the twenty-first century's open questions concerning the unity of mathematical knowledge.

Available in Japanese, English, French, German, Russian, and Turkish.
