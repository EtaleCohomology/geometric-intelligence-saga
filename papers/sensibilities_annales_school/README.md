# The History of Sensibilities and the Annales School

*The Geometry of Sensibilities: On the Connection between the History of Sensibilities of the Annales School and the Theory of Geometric Intelligence.*

## Overview

The present subdirectory contains the four linguistic versions of a single methodological proposal: the application of the Riemannian-geometric framework of Geometric Intelligence (GI) theory to the *histoire des sensibilités* — the history of sensibilities — as inaugurated by the third generation of the French Annales school.

The paper addresses the qualitative historical scholarship of Alain Corbin (the olfactory history of eighteenth- and nineteenth-century France; the soundscape of the nineteenth-century countryside; the history of attitudes to the shore), of Georges Vigarello (the history of cleanliness and the body), and of Roselyne Rey (the history of pain), and proposes three methodological pathways for the *mise en variété* of qualitative sources: embedding by means of a transformer-based language model; manual coding by the historian, in the tradition of Michel Vovelle's Provençal wills study; and the combination of the two under a principle of triangulation.

A proof of concept upon synthetic data — twelve quartiers of Paris across fifteen decadal periods from 1750 to 1900, with eight variables drawn from Corbin's own conceptual framework — establishes the computational feasibility of the method. The principal finding of the Lie-derivative analysis is that Haussmann's *grands travaux* produced a degree of olfactory structural transformation 2.74 times that of the construction of the sewers considered in isolation, and the Pasteurian revolution a factor of 1.75 — both results standing in accord with Corbin's thesis that olfactory experience is, in its essence, a socially constituted phenomenon.
