# Historiography

*The Historian's Manifold: A Dialogue between E. H. Carr and Riemannian Geometry.*

## Overview

The present subdirectory contains the four linguistic versions of a single work: a philosophical dialogue between the historiographical tradition of E. H. Carr, the Annales school, and Benedict Anderson on the one hand, and the Riemannian-geometric apparatus of Geometric Intelligence (GI) theory on the other. The work constitutes a methodological reflection upon the interpretative dependence of the historical manifold — the thesis, namely, that identical data, under alteration of the objective function, give rise to geometrically distinct manifolds, and that the historian's vantage-point, in the sense of Carr, corresponds term for term to the objective function in the sense of GI theory.

The dialogue is carried by the characters of the author's GI case-study novels — Dr. Liluru, Professor Kurata, Kaito, Maki Endō, Rin, and Riem — assembled in Dr. Liluru's rooms at Trinity College, Cambridge, in the autumn of 2036. The argument proceeds through four successive formulations: first, the correspondence of Carr's historian's question to the objective function of GI theory; second, the extension of this correspondence to the choice of the Morse function upon the manifold, whereby history is shown to be doubly interpretation-dependent; third, the multiplicity of manifolds arising from multiple simultaneous objective functions, offered as a practical response to the Gödelian incompleteness theorem; and fourth, the structural identity of the collective mentality (*mentalité collective*) of the Annales school, the imagined community of Benedict Anderson, and the manifold of GI theory as three languages for a single phenomenon.

The work contains no new numerical computations. It is, throughout, a conceptual reinterpretation of the existing mathematical apparatus of GI theory — objective functions, manifolds, scalar curvature, Lie derivatives, Morse functions — in the light of twentieth-century historiography.

## Place in the GI Corpus

The present work is the first side story (*bangai-hen*) of the GI Case-Study Novel series. It is set in the autumn of 2036, half a year after the events of the novel *Riem's Error*, during the quiet interval of intellectual reconstruction that followed the completion of the Eight Eyes manifold extension. Familiarity with *Riem's Error* is not presupposed: the philosophical argument is self-contained, though references to Riem's Congolese experience — to Amani, to Kayamba — gain their full resonance only from the preceding novel.

## Genre

Historiographical reflection in novelistic form. The work belongs to the genre of the philosophical dialogue, after the manner of Plato, Galileo, and, more recently, Imre Lakatos's *Proofs and Refutations*.
