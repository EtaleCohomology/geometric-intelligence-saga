# Sacred Music

*The Application of Geometric Intelligence Theory to the Corpus of Sacred Music.*

## Overview

The present directory gathers the case studies in which the Riemannian-geometric framework of Geometric Intelligence (GI) theory is applied to works of sacred music. Music — and most particularly the liturgical music of the Western Christian tradition — furnishes, it may be said, one of the purest available instances of a *sensible space*: a domain wherein the most delicate structures of human feeling have been, over the course of centuries, inscribed and transmitted with a precision scarcely equalled elsewhere.

The methodological ambition of this directory is twofold. In the first instance, it seeks to demonstrate that the mathematical apparatus developed for the analysis of economic, political, and historical structures — the pullback metric of a variational autoencoder decoder, the Lie derivative, the scalar curvature, the geodesic equation — admits of meaningful application to musical scores. In the second, it proposes that such application, far from reducing music to its numerical skeleton, may on the contrary render visible, to the analytical eye, structures that lie beneath the perceptual surface — structures of which performers and listeners are, as a rule, implicitly aware without being able to articulate them.

## Subdirectories

### `ave_verum_corpus/`

Mozart's motet *Ave Verum Corpus* (K. 618, June 1791), composed six months before the composer's death for the feast of Corpus Christi at the small parish of Baden-bei-Wien. The subdirectory contains two studies: the first a methodological proposal upon semi-synthetic data, the second an empirical verification upon data derived from the MIDI transcription of the published score (IMSLP). Available in Japanese, in English, in German, in French, and in Latin.
