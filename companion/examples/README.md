# Case Studies

Complete implementation examples demonstrating the ten-step pipeline of the companion volume of Geometric Intelligence (GI) theory.

## Purpose

Each case study applies the full pipeline — from data ingestion through final governance decision — to a fictional but plausible corporate or policy scenario. The data is synthetic, generated from known latent structures so that the geometric analysis can be verified against ground truth. The cases are designed to show how the mathematical machinery of GI theory translates into concrete managerial insight.

## Scope

These are not toy problems. Each case study includes realistic indicator sets (typically 40-50 variables across 6-10 categories), 10-year monthly time series, and the full set of quality checks that the companion volume specifies. The aim is reproducibility: a reader who follows the pipeline should obtain results consistent with those reported.

## Current Cases

### `case_study_01_yamato_foods_*`

*Yamato Foods Inc. — retort food manufacturer, six-market expansion decision.*

A fictional Tokyo-based retort food manufacturer considering entry into the Indian market. 50 indicators across 8 categories, 6 markets, 10 years of monthly data (720 observations). The pipeline produces: latent manifold construction, pullback metric, scalar curvature map identifying a structural transition zone between Japan/USA and India/Brazil clusters, Lie derivative analysis of three candidate policies, and an optimal control schedule over 30 months. The board resolution adopts the geometrically-optimal path with explicit risk buffer allocation.

Files:
- `case_study_01_yamato_foods_{JA,EN}.pdf` — pipeline execution report
- `case_study_01_yamato_foods_data_{JA,EN}.pdf` — dataset details (indicators, synthetic SNS text, fictional media headlines, fictional regulatory documents)

## On Synthetic Data

All data in these case studies is synthetic. Company names, SNS posts, media headlines, and regulatory documents are fabricated for illustrative purposes. Real institutional names (Nikkei, Bloomberg, FDA, FSSAI, etc.) are used as source labels, but the documents themselves are fictional. This is stated explicitly in each case study.

Synthetic data allows us to verify geometric analyses against known ground truth, which is the primary reason for using it in pedagogical material.
