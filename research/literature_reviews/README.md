# literature_reviews/

Surveys of research related to Geometric Intelligence (GI) theory.

## Purpose

GI theory does not exist in isolation. It sits at the intersection of geometric deep learning, information geometry, optimal transport, manifold learning, differential geometry, and control theory on manifolds. This subdirectory documents how GI theory relates to these fields — what it shares, what it differs in, what it can learn from them.

The reviews here inform both `mathematical_foundations/` (what frameworks to evaluate) and `python_implementations/` (what to prototype). They also serve as a record of due diligence: every claim that GI theory makes about its novelty can be traced back to a concrete comparison with prior work.

## Scope

Typical content:

- Systematic comparison with Bronstein et al. (2021), *Geometric Deep Learning*
- Position relative to Amari's information geometry
- Relationship to Villani's optimal transport theory
- Connection to Lawrence's GPLVM
- Relation to Chen et al. (2018), *Neural ODE*
- Commentary on Mochizuki's inter-universal Teichmüller theory, Maruyama Yoshihiro's categorical unified science, and the Langlands programme as mathematical horizons
- Survey of applied work: Ollivier-Ricci curvature on financial networks, symplectic geometry in economics, Poincaré embeddings for hierarchical data

## Files

Files will be added as reviews are completed. Each review opens with a summary of the prior work, states its relation to GI theory, and notes implications for the current programme.

## Method

Reviews aim for neutrality. The purpose is to understand what has been done and to identify what remains unaddressed. Criticism is directed at specific technical gaps, not at the value of prior work.
